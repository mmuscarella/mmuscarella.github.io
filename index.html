Welcome to my Research Page

I am a 5th year graduate student at Ecology and Evolution at Indiana University in the Lennon Lab (Lennon Lab Website; @LennonLab). I use a variety of technique to explore the ecology and physiology of microorganisms.

I am broadly interested in the role of microorganisms in the environment and how environmental changes impact the structure and function of microbial communities. Currently, I am working with freshwater bacterial communities and how they are responding to changes in carbon resources due to the Browning Phenomenon. My research uses a mixture of field observations, laboratory experimentation, and genomic analysis. Learn more about my active research here.

In addition to my research, I take part in various teaching responsibilities and outreach activities, and I am also a bit of a computer geek and enjoy learning about and working with various tools to analyze and interpret data in unique ways.

To learn more about me and my interests, please feel free to follow me on twitter (@AquaticMicrobes) and GitHub (mmuscarella).
